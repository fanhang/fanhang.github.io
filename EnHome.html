About Me

I am a lecturer and master supervisor at the School of Economics and Management, North China Electric Power University. I received my B.E. degree from the School of Electrical Engineering (Wu Yuzhang Honors College), Sichuan University in June 2015, and my Ph.D. degree from the Department of Electrical Engineering, Tsinghua University in June 2021, under the supervision of Prof. Shengwei Mei. During my doctoral study, I visited the School of Engineering and Applied Sciences at Harvard University and completed Tsinghua University's graduate data science training program.

From September 2021 to September 2023, I was a postdoctoral fellow at the PBC School of Finance, Tsinghua University, advised by Prof. Li Liao and jointly trained with Prof. Wei Xu at the Institute for Interdisciplinary Information Sciences. My research sits at the intersection of electrical engineering, artificial intelligence, finance, and energy economics, with an emphasis on electricity markets, renewable energy forecasting, privacy-preserving computation, and trusted circulation of energy data assets.

I have led or participated in more than ten grid and AI-related research projects, published or accepted more than 40 SCI/EI and core journal papers, obtained nine authorized invention patents, and authored or contributed to three books on data economy and electricity markets.
